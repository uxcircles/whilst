The question behind the numbers

Your spending is not just a record of what you bought.
It is a record of who you are becoming.

Whilst records where your money goes — and converts every purchase into what it actually cost you: hours of your finite life, days with the people who matter, distance from the thing you said you most wanted.

Not to judge. Not to optimise. To make the invisible, visible.

What it does
01
It asks you one honest question

Before anything else: what is the one thing in your life you most dread missing? Not what you should want. Not what sounds noble. Everything that follows is calibrated to that answer.

02
It converts money into time

Every purchase is translated into hours of your life exchanged. And not just working hours — days remaining with the people you love most. The number is rarely comfortable.

03
It holds up a mirror, monthly

Once a month, a single question. Not about budgets. About the life you are actually living versus the one you said you wanted. Your answers belong to no one else.

04
It remembers when you forget

Seven days after a purchase made whilst escaping something, Whilst asks: do you even remember buying it? The answer tells you more than the amount ever could.
